International Students Face Extra Struggles to Find Housing in NYC

October 6, 2022

One of the biggest challenges international students face in New York City is finding housing while they are adjusting to moving into a new country. Part of the higher education institutions do not provide dorms or off-campus housing solutions for their students, which forces internatoinals to navigate an unfamiliar situation on their own, without help or support. According to NYC official data as of 2017 “citywide rental vacancy rate is 3.63%, below the 5% threshold required for rent regulation to continue under State law. This translates into the availability of just 79,190 vacant units out of almost 2.2 million rental units citywide”, which creates an extremely high level of competition on apartments in New York City.

Since the demand on apartments is high, many of the landlords and building management companies comfortably tend to prefer leasing their properties to citizens of USA or non-citizens who have a sufficient and stable income and credit score, rather than leasing to international students who usually have limited to no income, regardless of whether they have decent savings, grants or other means to pay the rent. In many cases, that leaves students with little options of apartments with less competition, which can be distant from school, in unsafe areas or of extremely poor living standards.
